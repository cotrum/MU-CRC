Phishing Awareness

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber-attack method used to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as login credentials, financial details, or personal data. Attackers often disguise themselves as trusted entities through emails, websites, or messages to deceive victims.

Common Phishing Techniques

  • Email Phishing: Fraudulent emails appearing from legitimate sources.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing attacks on specific individuals or organizations.
  • Whaling: Phishing attacks aimed at high-profile executives.
  • Smishing: Phishing attacks via SMS messages.
  • Vishing: Voice phishing through phone calls.
  • Clone Phishing: Duplicate versions of legitimate messages with malicious links.

How to Recognize Phishing Attempts

  • Unusual sender email addresses
  • Spelling and grammar errors
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Suspicious links or attachments
  • Requests for sensitive information
  • Unexpected emails from known contacts

How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify sender identities before responding
  • Hover over links to check URLs before clicking
  • Use multi-factor authentication (MFA)
  • Keep software and security patches up to date
  • Educate yourself and others about phishing threats
  • Report suspected phishing attempts to IT or security teams

Famous Phishing Attacks

  • Google and Facebook Scam (2013-2015) - $100M stolen via fake invoices
  • Yahoo Data Breach (2013-2014) - 3 billion accounts compromised
  • Target Data Breach (2013) - 40M credit card numbers stolen
  • Twitter Bitcoin Scam (2020) - High-profile accounts hacked
